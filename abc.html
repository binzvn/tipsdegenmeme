Before you dive in, understand the commitment and capital required. These are the non-negotiable prerequisites for survival and success.

  • Crypto Basics: Solid understanding of crypto, CEX trading, and blockchain is assumed.
  • Meme Awareness: Be familiar with meme/degen projects and how they operate across chains (ETH, BSC, SOL).
  • Strong Mindset: A project can die overnight. You need dedication and a cool head.

Understanding the Playing Field

Meme coin trading is pure speculation. Know the different types of plays you can make, each with its own risk profile.

1. Alpha Gem (Cabals)

Projects with a dedicated team actively pushing the price. The most promising for reliable growth if you get in early, but very difficult to find.

2. Community Take Over (CTO)

Coins supported primarily by the community, often with a good story or avatar. Easiest to get involved with; strength depends on the community.

3. KOLs/Celebrity Mentions

Coins born from a post by an influencer (e.g., Elon Musk). This is the ultimate high-risk, high-reward play, with potential for 10-100x in minutes.

Core Trading Strategies

Knowing when to enter and exit is everything. This playbook covers fundamental strategies and capital management rules.

When to Buy?

Intuition Play

If a project seems interesting, has a good story, or a compelling avatar - sometimes it's that simple. Trust your gut (but be ready to cut).

KOL/Narrative Play

If a major influencer mentions a coin, buy immediately if the price is good or you spot a decent dip. Speed is key.

Insider/Team Play

If you know it's a project from a large, competent team, you can buy and hold, following their strategy and wallet movements.

When to Sell?

The 3-Minute Rule

For low-cap degen plays, if there's no volume or positive movement right after you buy, sell immediately. Don't hesitate.

Momentum Check

For KOL-driven coins, observe the momentum. Check daily volume and see if a community is forming on Twitter. If momentum fades, get out.

Follow the Smart Money

For team-backed projects, monitor the team's wallets. If they start selling, it's probably time for you to sell too.

Capital Management Rules

  • Unit Size: Risk only a small fraction of your capital per trade. (e.g., 0.25 SOL per trade on a 2 SOL bankroll).
  • Stop-Loss: Mentally (or physically, if possible) set a stop-loss at -30% to -50%. Respect it.
  • Cut Losses Quickly: Don't hold losing tokens. Your capital is better deployed elsewhere.
  • Avoid FOMO: Only buy if you realistically see 2-5x potential from the current price. Don't chase pumps.
  • Hunt Low-Caps: With small capital (<10 SOL), focus on tokens under $250k market cap for higher growth potential.

The Survivor's Mindset

95% of memecoins go to zero. These are the rules the profitable traders live by. Internalize them.

Assume It Goes to Zero

Trade like every single memecoin you buy will eventually be worthless. Because it probably will.

Don't Be a Community Member

You are a trader, not a fan. Don't "marry" a token or get emotionally attached. Your goal is profit, not friendship.

Take Profits & Move On

Secure your gains and immediately look for the next opportunity. Greed kills. There are 3,000+ new pairs a day.

Stack Small Wins

Accumulate consistent 50-80% profits. Don't hold out for a 100x on every trade. Small, steady gains build your bankroll.

Find Your Wolfpack

Trade with a trusted group of friends. This reduces your risk of being used as "exit liquidity" for others.

Be Patient With Capital

Don't increase your unit size or get reckless as you win. Stay disciplined. Euris didn't change his unit size until 100 SOL.
